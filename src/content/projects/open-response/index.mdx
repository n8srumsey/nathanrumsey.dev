## Context & Problem Statement
Open Response addresses a critical gap in modern education: the high cost of classroom engagement tools. Commercial polling software like Top Hat and Poll Everywhere creates financial barriers for both students and professors, limiting access to real-time classroom interaction. 

The project originated with Dr. Evan Thatcher, a Senior Physics Instructor at Oregon State University, who conceived the idea to eliminate these costs. While development began in 2021, progress stalled until 2024, when the project was handed to our team in an incomplete state with significant technical debt, broken functionality, and an unpolished legacy interface. We took on the task of rewriting major sections of the project codebase, as the project scope, features, and needs have evolved significantly since the idea was first conceived of in 2021.

## Impact & Accessibility
Open Response empowers educational institutions and school districts to self-host and customize the platform to their specific needs. By eliminating licensing fees while maintaining feature parity with paid solutions, we've democratized access to real-time classroom analytics and engagement tools. The platform now supports instructors in fostering participation, adapting teaching strategies based on live insights, and tracking student performance over time, all without financial burden on students.

## Team & Timeline
Developed from September 2024 to June 2025 by a six-person Agile team (Sean Gibson, Sage Morgillo, Tran Cong Son Nguyen, Nathan Rumsey, Nathaniel Wood, and Evan Baumann) to realize Dr. Evan Thatcher's vision. The project represents a successful handoff and completion of a multi-year software development initiative, transforming an incomplete prototype into a deployable, production-ready platform.

## Our Solution
As a six-person team, we transformed Open Response into a fully functional, open-source classroom polling platform that delivers enterprise-grade features at zero cost. Our work focused on three core pillars:

**Technical Modernization**: 
We completely redesigned the system architecture, transitioning from a monolithic design to a distributed architecture, composed of a core backend API and a separate lecture service to support real-time responsiveness. We leveraged Redis as a distributed cache to support the near-instantaneous interactivity that real-time features demanded, rather than relying on a MySQL database. These shifts enabled scalable deployment, improved fault tolerance, and real-time interactivity. We also rebuilt the student question interface, replacing the legacy UI with a polished, user-friendly design that significantly enhanced usability and accessibility.

**Feature Completion**: 
From September 2024 to June 2025, we delivered all core capabilities that instructors expect from commercial alternatives:
- Real-time question delivery and instant response recording
- Flexible assessment tools with diverse question formats and customizable scoring
- Automated grading with immediate student feedback
- Integrated gradebook with CSV export to Learning Management Systems such as Canvas, Google Classroom, and more
- Course and roster management

**Quality Assurance**: 
We established a robust software testing suite to prevent future regression, ensuring that new development wouldn't break existing features or degrade code quality. This foundation supports long-term maintainability for future teams.
